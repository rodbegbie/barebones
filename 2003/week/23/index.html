Take (Secure) AIM

The latest beta version of AIM finally includes the ability to hold encrypted conversations between suitably certified parties.

The problem is that it’s still a complete bugger to attempt to get going. You have to jump through all manner of hoops just to get a certificate, then there’s the pain of importing it into AIM through a not terribly useable interface.

Which is a shame, because more people should have access to this kind of thing; It should be one-click installable. I have to go through a bunch of high-level geekery (gawd bless SSH(Secure Shell)) to encrypt all my web-surfing, emailing and IMing at work, because I don’t trust the corporate sysadmins not to be spying on what I’m up to. (And any company that blocks FuckedCompany.com at the firewall, can, quite frankly, go become a fucked company).
