iTunes for Windows

iTunes for Windows. Hurrah!

First impressions: Works almost identically to the Mac version (some slight bugs with Smart Playlists), which is a very good thing.

I’m very excited to finally be able to share my music between PCs. Will be very handy at home for me & Joy to ransack each other’s collections, and could be fun at work.

I’m looking forward to nuking MusicMatch off of Joy’s PC later. It really is a god-awful piece of crap, compared to the shiny reliable iTunes. I don’t think we’ve managed to sync up her iPod once since the initial load.

It’s kind of sad, though, that while the CxO of AOL was on Apple’s stage boasting about AOL linking into the iTunes Music Store, the Nullsoft division of AOL chose today to release the first beta of Winamp 5. As if anyone’s going to notice.

➠ October 14, 2003

nOMENCLATURE

Since Joy has changed her name, I thought it was time I put an end to an old and ultimately pointless joke of my own. I’m getting back into the habit of typing my name with correct capitalisation.

The “rOD” thing started at my old consulting gig. My employers went through an extensive (and frankly, much needed) rebranding exercise. Huge screeds of documentation were produced on how to apply the new brand. In a suitably satyrical stylee, I announced to my team that I was rebranding myself, and they should follow my new guidelines on how to write my name.

Over the next few months, I took advantage of various opportunities to “adjust” internal systems within the company to comply. An unattended server in the server room allowed me to change my outgoing email. The well-known System password for the HR database (“garfield”, in case they still haven’t changed it) allowed me to show up correctly on the intranet. Quite frankly, the only reason I didn’t change the payroll system when I had the chance was the fear of screwing up my paychecks.

But I was laid-off from that job over two years ago (perhaps unsurprisingly, given my confessions in the previous paragraph), and I kept the joke alive out of laziness. So time’s up—rOD is dead, long live Rod.

Clobbering Commentspam

Amongst the 199 messages in my Inbox upon getting home (not counting all the mailing list stuff filtered away, and the 427 spams caught by SpamAssassin) were a bunch of emails from MovableType telling me comments had been posted to my blog. Frustratingly, most of these were spammers trying to abuse blogs to garner precious PageRank.

I spent a wee while this morning pondering on ways to block the spammybastards. Then I loaded Bloglines and found someone had already done all the hard work for me. MT-Blacklist—Essential if you’re running MT and being clobbered by commentspam too.
