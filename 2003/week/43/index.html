Glider

You may have noticed a new symbol appear at the bottom of the right-column of my page. It’s esr’s proposed new “Hacker emblem”

The Linux folks have their penguin and the BSDers their daemon. Perl’s got a camel, FSF
fans have their gnu and OSI‘s got an open-source logo. What we haven’t had, historically, is an emblem that represents the entire hacker community of which all these groups are parts. This is a proposal that we adopt one — the glider pattern from the Game of Life.

Hi Jack

Megawickedawesome app of the day: Audio Hijack. Truly a thing of beauty. It syphons off the audio from applications like Real Player and stores them on your hard drive.

Sure, there’ve been hacks to do this on other OSes for ages, but none do it as stylishly as Audio Hijack. It includes a scheduler to record from “the radio” when you command, and can automagically compress it to an AAC file and add it to your iTunes library.

This morning, I awoke to find a rerun of Just a Minute ready to sync onto my iPod for the journey to work. Hurrah! Well worth the $16 registration.

“Let’s Necklace Dissent”

Games Gone Wild!—The latest games news from David Cross.

One supersecret game that’s been on everyone’s hot and new list is Extreme Special Olympics, by the folks at EA Sports. This one’s self-explanatory. You control one of four athletes, each with a distinct personality and skill set. My favorites: Daphne, who believes she can fly (she can’t, and this comes into play later in the game), and Mickey, who has a mild form of autism and the ability to avoid hugs.
