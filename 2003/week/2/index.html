➠ January 18, 2003

Hitler never played Risk

I’ve lost far too many hours today to playing iConquer, an OSX implementation of Risk.

I only played Risk once before in my life—And I remember really enjoying it. This implementation is fantastic. The author has taken great care to make it look pretty, and the computer players are brilliantly written too.

I forked out my $12 registration fee after half-an-hours play. (And if anyone fancies challenging me to a network game, my AIM ID is rODonHisMac)

Glass Slipper

Huge hearty thanks to Mark for pointing out Syndirella, a new RSS reader for Windows that is bloody brilliant. It’s open-sourced and written in C#, and the code is so better organised than Feedreader. I think I have a new favourite.

Update: Two hours later, I broke out Visual Studio, and added two features to the program (Changing the display font, and allowing me to double click on a headline and have it open up in Phoenix). Damn, I love open-source.

My Maciversary

Clearing out some old mail folders, I realised that it’s exactly one year since I bought my first-ever piece of Apple hardware: A 5Gb iPod.

It really was the proverbial Trojan Horse. Since then, I’ve purchased a second-hand G3 Blue & White, upgraded to a 20Gb iPod, then splashed out a cool $2k on a super-shiny, sleek and sexy G4.

I watch Steve Jobs’s keynotes with an element of excitement. I ooh and aah at his demonstrations, and then when I get my hands on the items in question, I ooh and aah again (Seriously—I wanted to wake Joy up last night to show her how incredible One Click Enhance is!).

And then this weekend, I sold off the old G3. The purchaser was a guy who’d received an iPod as a Christmas present. And so the cycle starts again.
