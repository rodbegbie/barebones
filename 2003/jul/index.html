➠ July 27, 2003

Out of Focus

There’s a discussion on Slashdot about a subject of great interest to me at the moment: How do you stay focused and get your work done when you’re only one click away from the Internet?

My main problem is that my work laptop is two and a half years old, with a slow hard drive, and no way to upgrade the memory. Switching between my web browser and IDE (which you need to do a lot when you’re building a web application) can take 30 seconds, which gives my mind plenty of time to forget the task in hand and wander onto the twenty other topics swimming around in there.

For me, the key is to make a list of things to be done on a post-it note next to my PC, and check things off as and when I get them done. There’s always a list there, so I can always pick something. Having a brain-freeze? Then I just pick something mundane and simple from the list (eg. fixing typos in the comments). Ready to get my head down and code, code, CODE!? Then pick a tougher one from the post-it and get stuck in.

Note: Since staring to write this entry, I have flipped between my text editor, my web-browser, a couple of SSH sessions and Soulseek about 10 times. Maybe there’s something in this ADHD thing…

➠ July 23, 2003

BookQueue

One of my favourite things about MovableType is its extendibility. Perlgeeks can churn out plugins which add all kinds of new and delicious groovyness to the platform.

My new favourite is the BookQueue Plugin, which I installed last night. My reading list is now proudly displayed on the right, but what’s brilliant about it is that I only had to enter the books’ ISBNs—The plugin ransacked Amazon’s database, and got all the relevant juicy details such as title and author.

And since it’s all in lovely open Perl code, I was able to hack it to do lookups against amazon.co.uk when nothing was found on the US site. Result!

I also hereby pledge that you will never see one of these book covers appear on the list.

Block the Flash

Yet another reason to love the lizard: There’s a smashing wee Mozilla plugin called Flash Click To View. It replaces Flash animations with a button, which you can click to see the animation, thereby reducing the chances of some webpage blasting loud music when you’re meant to be “researching” (and reducing the number of “Shoot the Monkey” animated banner ads). Making the Bloglines

I’ve been using a news aggregator for a while now. I used to spend time each day checking round a load of websites in my bookmarks to see if they’ve been updated. A news aggregator saves you that work, by searching round for you, and listing the new entries in one application.

Now you can get all the loveliness of an aggregator, without the hassle of having to install anything on your PC. Mark Fletcher has just launched Bloglines, a web-based aggregator which will track all the blogs and news sites you can throw at it.

Go there, sign up for an account, then come back and click on the image below to subscribe to Groovymother:

It’s only a day-old, and still pretty rough around the edges, but it’s definitely a promising start. Highly recommended.
