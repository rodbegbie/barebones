More ramblings of a Caffinated Bent

My post the other day about the number of nearby Dunkin’ Donuts branches was inspired by the introduction of Dunkin’ Donuts rankings on boston-online.com.

However, over the weekend, I realised a flaw with this scoring. Whilst wandering near Boston Common, I noticed that each branch of DDs that I passed had a Starbucks, surely the antithesis of New England, right opposite it. This has to be taken into account when calculating your DD rating.

So, I propose that the correct way to calculate your DD rating is thusly:

((DD - SB) * 100) / DD

(where DD is “number of Dunkin’ Donuts branches within 5 miles”, and SB is “number of Starbucks branches within 5 miles”)

The range goes from 100 (if there are only DDs within your grasp) to negative infinity (if you live in a wholly Starbucks-populated zone).

My score is a still reasonably respectable 62.5DD.

Have I perhaps spent a smidge too much time thinking about this?
