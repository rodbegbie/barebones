T-Shirt Whore

Threadless.com screams my name with freakish accuracy.

Threadless allows anyone to upload a shirt design. The inertnet community votes and comments on these designs. Then once a month, they pick a handful of the top-scorers and turn them into available-to-buy limited-run honest-to-goodness t-shirts.

I’m a total t-shirt whore. I freely admit that I have far more shirts than I could ever wear, and a (sadly) ever-increasing number of them no longer fit due to my (sadly) ever-increasing belly. But still I hold on to them, in the vague hope that I will one day be slender enough to squeeze my frame into that Size “M” Idler snail shirt I bought when I was still a (relatively) scrawny student.

Brilliant stuff.

Syndirella — Drag’n’Drop Feed Reordering — UPDATED

Building upon my work from the other day, I now present my latest hacked-on layer for Syndirella—Drag’n’drop feed re-ordering.

Shift the order of your feed list by dragging and dropping news feeds. Added bonus: You can drag links from IE or Mozilla into the feed list, and Syndirella will subscribe to them. Try dragging in my RSS feed to see it in action.

Known Issues:

  • The feed list doesn’t scroll when you get near the edge
  • You can’t double-click on a Feed and go to its homepage.
  • Dragging from the area below the feed list caused an exception
  • The cursor shows the “No Entry” sign when you drag links from IE. Don’t believe it! It’ll work!

Syndirella — Now with added Toast

Syndirella is in a rather unpleasant state of limbo at the moment. The original author’s stated that he pretty much wants to give up development, and has been reasonably unreachable of late.

So here’s a release of a change I’ve wanted for a while. This release include pop-up “toast”, as seen in apps such as MSN Messenger or DeadAIM, alerting you to new stories.

These were implemented using code from The Genghis Project.

Known issues/TODOs:

  • Only one piece of toast can display at a time (though this may be a feature, since if 20 blogs updated, you’d fill your screen pretty quickly)
  • There’s no option to turn it off. There is now!
  • You can’t click on a piece of toast and restore the main window. You can now!
  • The copyright statement for the Genghis Project hasn’t been added anywhere. Consider this part of the documentation: Portions copyright 2002–2003 The Genghis Group (http://www.genghisgroup.com/).

More ramblings of a Caffinated Bent

My post the other day about the number of nearby Dunkin’ Donuts branches was inspired by the introduction of Dunkin’ Donuts rankings on boston-online.com.

However, over the weekend, I realised a flaw with this scoring. Whilst wandering near Boston Common, I noticed that each branch of DDs that I passed had a Starbucks, surely the antithesis of New England, right opposite it. This has to be taken into account when calculating your DD rating.

So, I propose that the correct way to calculate your DD rating is thusly:

((DD - SB) * 100) / DD

(where DD is “number of Dunkin’ Donuts branches within 5 miles”, and SB is “number of Starbucks branches within 5 miles”)

The range goes from 100 (if there are only DDs within your grasp) to negative infinity (if you live in a wholly Starbucks-populated zone).

My score is a still reasonably respectable 62.5DD.

Have I perhaps spent a smidge too much time thinking about this?
