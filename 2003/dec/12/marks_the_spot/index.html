Marks The Spot

Robert X. Cringely asks the question which I’ve been confused about for a while… Instead of all this nonsense about touch-screen voting machines, and hanging chads, why not just draw an X on a bit of paper and count it by hand? It’s worked in the UK for years.

On Friday, December 12, 2003, adamg commented:

No, you don't want to go down that route! Dunno about Massachusetts, but New York State has whole volumes of legislation and case law relating to hand-marked ballots and all the things that can and have been done to and with them - my father's made a pretty penny as an election-law lawyer there.

Boston now has OCR-type voting (you fill in ovals, like some sort of standardized test, then push it into a ballot eater, um, reader, so you get both an automated count AND an audit trail of sorts). Biggest problem is not the ballots themselves but the fact that the voting "booths" are now these tiny little platforms that are just too open - anybody could be looking over your shoulder as you vote. Or anybody could watch your ballot as you put it in the machine. They have these sort of envelope things you can request for the latter, but when my wife did at the last election, they laughed at her.
