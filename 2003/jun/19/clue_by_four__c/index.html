Clue By Four! Clue By Four! CLUE BY FOUR!

➠ June 19, 2003

Clue By Four! Clue By Four! CLUE BY FOUR!

Received an email this morning:

Hello.

I found your web site,
http://www.groovymother.com/archives/week_2002_10_20.html has a reference to
a Clue-By-Four™. Unfortunately, my company owns the trademark to that
term, and I am in the process of bringing that product to market. My lawyers
have told me that if you do not remove that reference, it dilutes my
trademark.

I would much rather ask you politely to remove references to Clue-by-Four™
than have an ugly lawyerese letter sent via certified mail, etc.

Thank you for your consideration.

Joel McClung
President, Intrax, Inc.

I’ve given this a lot of thought, and here is my official opinion on the matter:

  1. Clue-by-four is a well-known phrase, which pre-dates the trademark registration by many years. Therefore, it should be considered a “generic term”.
  2. Bite me.
  3. If you seriously want the words removed from my website, pay your fucking lawyers, you cheap-ass bastard.
  4. Good luck on removing all references to “clue-by-fours” from the many copies of the Jargon Dictionary distributed since 12-Mar-1999 which would… oops… pre-date your trademark filing date of 18-Aug-1999.
  5. No, seriously. Bite me hard.
