The Impending Death of Audiogalaxy

The news that the RIAA is finally chasing after Audiogalaxy should come as a surprise to absolutely no-one. The interesting question is how the hell AG still exists!

I’ve been using the AG Satellite for well over two years, and AG’s FTP search for at least one more. They existed before Napster came along and made it as simple to find music as it was to share it.

They survived the dot-com bust, despite the fact that their revenue model was the same advertising-based concept that caused so many others to go boom.

And they managed to sneak under the RIAA‘s radar. While they were busy shutting down Napster, or chasing the relatively johnny-come-latelys like Morpheus or Kazaa, AG just kept their heads down, and implemented an easy-to-get-around lame filter.

When they’re brought down, I hope they just say “Goodnight” and die with dignity, instead of struggling along trying to keep the brand name alive. But they are still, for my money, the best way to find obscure tracks on the internet. And for that, I salute their copyright-ignoring pirate-flag-waving souls.
