Mini

I’ve spent the last week drooling over a new piece of technolust: The new MINI Cooper.

It looks like exactly what I’m after. Lots of BMW-style on-board computery wizardry, in a funky wee shell that isn’t too hard to park. I really quite fancy the idea of zipping down the Mass Pike in one.

I did like this review at Epinions…

Prospective MINI buyers fall into one of two categories: (A) Those shopping for a small, sporty coupe, sports sedan, roadster, or econo-car, and comparing features and prices with similar models (if you can find them); and (B) Those who saw the MINI and decided to buy it, whether they needed it or not.

Yep, I’m definitely Category B!

A Submarine

The BBC have published an article about the report that semen makes women happy. Apparently, women who engage in unbarriered sex are happier in life than those who “use protection”.

When discussing this with a friend a few weeks ago, she pointed out that it might not be quite so cause-and-effect. Her theory was that women who were bonking strangers unprotected are just idiots, so therefore will be happier with life because they know no better, while women who are sleeping with long-term partners with no protection clearly trust their partners, so have a cheerier outlook on life.

Whereas women using condoms every time are concerned about STDs and/or babies, so are just “worriers”—Hence, less happy.

But still, there could be something in it. Maybe the people behind ManNotIncluded.com should register CheaperThanProzac.com too?
