You may already have won

A while ago, I entered a competition with the first prize of a trip for four to L.A.

Today, I received an email telling me that I was a winner. Sadly, not of the grand prize, but of fourth prize. Sounds good, non?

Fourth Prize consists of 1 manufacturer's coupon redeemable at participating retailers for 1 free bag of 3D's(R) brand snacks (up to 7 oz. size), with an approximate retail value of $2.59 and an expiration date of 12/31/02.

Woo! A free bag of chips. In order to my receive my prize, however, I have to fill out a form, mail it in to Frito Lay’s and wait “6 to 8 weeks”.

Even better, the form asks me to provide my social security number and date of birth, then I have to sign to agree with the following:

By accepting the prize, I (and my parent/legal guardian if I am a minor) agree to release Frito-Lay, Inc. ("Sponsor"), FOX Broadcasting Company, 20th Century FOX Television, Regency Entertainment, their respective parent, subsidiary or affiliated companies, distributors, printing suppliers for this promotion, advertising, fulfillment, judging or promotion agencies, participating retailers, and all of their respective officers, directors, employees, representatives and agents from any liability for, and to hold them harmless against, any liability for injuries, losses or damages of any kind to persons or property resulting in whole or in part, directly or indirectly, from participation in the sweepstakes or acceptance, possession, misuse or use of any prize.

If the chips are so bad that they’re worried it might cause me “injuries, losses or damages” then I’m really not sure I want them.

Dougie! He’s got a banana in his hand!

In the middle of a Grauniad article on Yahoo, I was struck by a quote:

"The Yahoo brand has become confused over time. [...] Compare it to Google, which ultimately works best as a search engine. It's quick and it does exactly what it says on the tin."

“Does exactly what it says on the tin” is a a phrase originally used in a TV advert for bleeding wood stain. After being used for years in popular culture (usually in some completely wanky set-up such as “As the Ronseal guy might say, the new Dumpy’s Rusty Nuts CD does exactly what it says on the tin”), it’s apparently now a common colloquial phrase!

Indeed, politicians are saying it in a desperate attempt to appear non-fuddy-duddy, and the compilers of the Oxford English Dictionary now list it!

So, please join me in wishing to kill off the expression now. Perhaps replacing it with another commercial tagline from the same period? “It’s slightly rippled with a flat underside”, anyone?

Another Google Bomb

Textism is attempting to create a Google Bomb to highlight Verisign being bureaucratic dimwits. However, I believe it is doomed to failure, for one simple reason:

Verisign is already heavily linked to in Google’s database. The reason previous Google Bombs (such as “talentless hack” or “Internet rockstar”) were successful was that there wasn’t already a high-PageRanking entry for those search-terms.

For example, Verisign.com already has “Big Important Websites” pointing to it which’ll score more highly than a handful of piddly blogs. Oh, and all the links in the first page of results bar “netsol.com” have verisign in the domain name.

But what the hey! I’m happy to do my part (and would really quite like to be proved wrong), so: Verisign.

Belle and Sebastian Rock My World

I saw Belle & Sebastian (one of my all-time most favouritist bands in the world of all time) last night at the Boston Orpheum.

Highlights include an audience member performing Kim Deal’s part in a cover of “Gigantic” (to which she knew, by her own admission, ”most of the words”), and a rawk-out ending to Lazy Line Painter Jane.

Yes, that’s right. B&S actually grew some great big hairy rawk testicles and cranked up the guitars to, if not eleven, at least eight-and-a-half.

The set covered their entire catalogue, although if I were to list all the tracks I wished they’d played, that’d take up a two-hour set too.

Be dazzled by my lack of photographic skill whilst viewing some photos from the gig.
