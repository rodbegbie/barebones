Dougie! He’s got a banana in his hand!

In the middle of a Grauniad article on Yahoo, I was struck by a quote:

"The Yahoo brand has become confused over time. [...] Compare it to Google, which ultimately works best as a search engine. It's quick and it does exactly what it says on the tin."

“Does exactly what it says on the tin” is a a phrase originally used in a TV advert for bleeding wood stain. After being used for years in popular culture (usually in some completely wanky set-up such as “As the Ronseal guy might say, the new Dumpy’s Rusty Nuts CD does exactly what it says on the tin”), it’s apparently now a common colloquial phrase!

Indeed, politicians are saying it in a desperate attempt to appear non-fuddy-duddy, and the compilers of the Oxford English Dictionary now list it!

So, please join me in wishing to kill off the expression now. Perhaps replacing it with another commercial tagline from the same period? “It’s slightly rippled with a flat underside”, anyone?
