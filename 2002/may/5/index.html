Another Google Bomb

Textism is attempting to create a Google Bomb to highlight Verisign being bureaucratic dimwits. However, I believe it is doomed to failure, for one simple reason:

Verisign is already heavily linked to in Google’s database. The reason previous Google Bombs (such as “talentless hack” or “Internet rockstar”) were successful was that there wasn’t already a high-PageRanking entry for those search-terms.

For example, Verisign.com already has “Big Important Websites” pointing to it which’ll score more highly than a handful of piddly blogs. Oh, and all the links in the first page of results bar “netsol.com” have verisign in the domain name.

But what the hey! I’m happy to do my part (and would really quite like to be proved wrong), so: Verisign.

Belle and Sebastian Rock My World

I saw Belle & Sebastian (one of my all-time most favouritist bands in the world of all time) last night at the Boston Orpheum.

Highlights include an audience member performing Kim Deal’s part in a cover of “Gigantic” (to which she knew, by her own admission, ”most of the words”), and a rawk-out ending to Lazy Line Painter Jane.

Yes, that’s right. B&S actually grew some great big hairy rawk testicles and cranked up the guitars to, if not eleven, at least eight-and-a-half.

The set covered their entire catalogue, although if I were to list all the tracks I wished they’d played, that’d take up a two-hour set too.

Be dazzled by my lack of photographic skill whilst viewing some photos from the gig.
