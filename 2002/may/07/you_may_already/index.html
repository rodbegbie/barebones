You may already have won

A while ago, I entered a competition with the first prize of a trip for four to L.A.

Today, I received an email telling me that I was a winner. Sadly, not of the grand prize, but of fourth prize. Sounds good, non?

Fourth Prize consists of 1 manufacturer's coupon redeemable at participating retailers for 1 free bag of 3D's(R) brand snacks (up to 7 oz. size), with an approximate retail value of $2.59 and an expiration date of 12/31/02.

Woo! A free bag of chips. In order to my receive my prize, however, I have to fill out a form, mail it in to Frito Lay’s and wait “6 to 8 weeks”.

Even better, the form asks me to provide my social security number and date of birth, then I have to sign to agree with the following:

By accepting the prize, I (and my parent/legal guardian if I am a minor) agree to release Frito-Lay, Inc. ("Sponsor"), FOX Broadcasting Company, 20th Century FOX Television, Regency Entertainment, their respective parent, subsidiary or affiliated companies, distributors, printing suppliers for this promotion, advertising, fulfillment, judging or promotion agencies, participating retailers, and all of their respective officers, directors, employees, representatives and agents from any liability for, and to hold them harmless against, any liability for injuries, losses or damages of any kind to persons or property resulting in whole or in part, directly or indirectly, from participation in the sweepstakes or acceptance, possession, misuse or use of any prize.

If the chips are so bad that they’re worried it might cause me “injuries, losses or damages” then I’m really not sure I want them.
