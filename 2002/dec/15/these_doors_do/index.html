These Doors Do Not Recycle

Following on from my “Random Signs Seen Around Cambridge” post from the other day, I spotted this rather intriguing notice on a bunch of doors on trains on the Red Line here in Boston. Haven’t the faintest clue what it means.

On Tuesday, June 24, 2003, patty commented:

i actually wrote the t about the "these doors do not recycle" stickers. here's their response:

Dear Ms. Butler,

Thank you for email inquiring about Red Line vehicles.

The sticker in question "THESE DOORS DO NOT RECYCLE" are displayed on
approximately 20 Red Line vehicles that are not equipped with sensitive
edges, which automatically detect an obstruction in the door and re-open
them.
There is an ongoing program to retrofit these vehicles with sensitive
edges. During a similar program on the Green Line these stickers were
utilized until the program was complete.

We are in the process of changing the wording and will replace the
current sticker. The terminology is common to us, and sometimes we
forget that transportation, as well as other industries, use their own
terminology that might not be clear cut to non-transportation people.

If you have any questions or concerns regarding Red Line service,
please
contact me at redline@mbta.com.

Sincerely,

Maureen Shirkus
Chief, Red Line Operations

45 High St. 10th Floor
Boston, MA 02110
