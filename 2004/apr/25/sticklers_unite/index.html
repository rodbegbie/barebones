➠ April 25, 2004

Sticklers’ Unite!

I’ve just found out that the thoroughly excellent Eats, Shoots & Leaves has now received its US publication.

I read the book upon its original UK publication, and loved it. It’s an ace combination of the history of punctuation (it never occured to me that at some point, someone had to invent the exclamation point), with guidelines on how to attempt to use it correctly—and where the controvery lies. (For example, I hate, despise and loathe the ”Oxford comma”, and refuse point-blank to use it.)

I’ve long been a grammar stickler—Indeed, when I started blogging again, my third post was obsessing about a semi-colon—and this book definitely helps me feel less alone. Want to find out how sticklery you are? Try the Punctuation Game; I’m a 92% stickler, apparently.

(Incidentally, the book’s title comes from a joke involving a panda bear entering a cafe, which is altogether cleaner than the original version of the joke that I know, where he visits a prostitute and it ain’t a gun that he shoots. I’ll let you all work that out for yourselves.)
