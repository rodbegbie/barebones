Putting my “reputation” on the line

Dan thinks that the just-announced Google Gmail service is an April Fool. I’m willing to be money that it’s not for a variety of reasons:

  1. It was put out on BusinessWire, AP, etc, and published in places like CNET and NYTimes. Nothing pisses off the media quite like (unknowingly) publishing a hoax.
  2. The Privacy Policy looks thoroughly legit to me. Just covering their posteriors about mailing service announcements, and displaying related ads.
  3. The Moon Base is a typical Google April Fools joke. Link on the homepage and silly “news” story. GMail, not so much.
  4. The MX entries for gmail.com shows different (non-functioning) SMTP servers than the MX entries for google.com.

I could end up with egg on my face for stating this publicly, but I reckon it’s totally legit.

UPDATE: Vindicated.
