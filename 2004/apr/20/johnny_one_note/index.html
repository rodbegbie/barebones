Johnny One Note

➠ April 20, 2004

Johnny One Note

I’ve long loved Microsoft OneNote. From its wobbly baby-steps during the Office 2003 beta program to the final-released copy, it’s Microsoft’s best new product in years.

If you’re unfamiliar, it’s an app which helps you organise unstructured/mildly structured notes. Perfect for taking notes during meetings, or capturing stuff off of the myriad of whiteboards I scrawl on on a daily basis.

The new OneNote SP1 Preview, released today, adds a bunch of new features, but none excite me quite so much as the ability to share out notepad pages across the network, and allow people to see what you’re writing, and make corrections in realtime.

Wiki-style networked virtual-whiteboarding with SubEthaEdit-esque editing in a Windows product? Sign me up now!

UPDATE: BTW, you don’t need to own a copy of OneNote to play with this new preview—Anyone can download, install and use for free until the end of August. Initial signs point to: It’s so fucking worth it!
