Apple’s UI Sucks

One of the advertised features of Apple’s iSync package is that you can use it with their ($99-a-year) .mac service to access your Address Book on the web when you need it.

The other day, I was in a meeting and needed to get a phone number. I didn’t have my Palm Pilot in the room, but have no fear, I knew I could access it quickly via the .mac service. Here’s just how quick and easy it was:

  1. Visit http://www.mac.com/
  2. Click on the nice big “Address Book” graphic at the top of the page.
  3. Get taken to a promotional page advertising the wonders of the .mac address book.
  4. Hunt around page for link to Login. (Ignoring the “Logout” link on the toolbar which displays whether you’re logged in or not!)
  5. Eventually find small “Already a member?” link in middle of promotional text imploring me to join.
  6. Click it.
  7. Get asked for username & password.
  8. Enter username & password.
  9. Get taken back to the .mac homepage. Not the Address Book as I might have hoped.
  10. Hunt around for (smaller) Address Book graphic, now in a completely different part of the screen from before.
  11. Click it.
  12. Get asked again for password.
  13. Enter password again.
  14. Finally get to a screen where I can access my phone book.

I suspect that Apple’s famed UI team don’t speak to the website folks much.
