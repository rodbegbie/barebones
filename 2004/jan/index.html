➠ January 28, 2004

-read now? [+ynq]

I’m very excited to finally be able to link today to Unison, the new OSX USENET client from Panic. It’s gorgeously designed to allow both message reading, and file downloading. This is real peer-to-peer file-sharing!

I’ve been running beta copies for a few months, and have been blown away by how easy they’ve made the horrendously geeky realm of the froup. Hunting for and grabbing large chunks of multimedia is a piece of piss—Getting thumbnails of all the images in an alt.binaries group is really handy.

Unison really is a beautiful example of the kind of design strides taken by people who are Macheads.

➠ January 26, 2004

PartitionMagic Kills Hard Drives

Far too much of my weekend was lost to my Windows PC, which completely died after I attempted to resize the partitions using PartitionMagic. Sadly, this is not the first time that PartitionMagic has fried a hard drive of mine. Fool me once, etc.

This was a real beauty of a crash too. It managed to scramble the hard drive so skillfully that every Windows installation CD I tried refused to start up while the hard drive was still connected! Thank gawd for my handy business card Linux CD which saved the day. Best $5(EFF Store: GNU-Linux Bootable Business Cards (BBC’s)) I ever spent. A quick parted(Parted – GNU Project – Free Software Foundation (FSF)) later, and I was off.

Best of all, my backup software (installed in a sleep deprived state) actually worked! Restoration was quick and painless.

And now, for my own reference next time I’m reinstalling Windows, here’s my current Must Install list—The very basics that I need, in order to have a fully functioning Windows desktop:

ReGet Deluxe, Cygwin, DAEMON Tools, WinZip, WinRAR, Firebird, Thunderbird, vim, TweakUI, PuTTY, FileZilla, RealVNC

Talkie Walkie

I’ve spent a large part of the last 24 hours gorging on the new Air album, Talkie Walkie. I’ve had it lying around on my hard drive for a month, but only got around to listening to it last night. I’m kicking myself now—It’s a truly beautiful piece of work.

It’s the first album I’ve fallen head-over-heels over on the first listen in months; Probably since Dear Catastrophe Waitress.

Go to the Astralwerks site and download “Alpha Beta Gaga”, a beautiful combination of strings, banjo and whistling. Then rush out and buy the album next Tuesday when it’s released—I know I will.

➠ January 12, 2004

A Little More Conversation

That line on my résumé saying that I’ve dabbled in Objective-C has become a little bit more true. I’ve just completed my first bit of Mac OS X coding: An update to the Audioscrobbler iTunes plugin.

Whilst building, I had some questions, and needed to hop onto the AudioScrobbler IRC channel. So I did a quick Google for a Mac IRC client, and came up with an absolute corker: Conversation.

I’ve used a bunch of IRC clients in my time, but Conversation blew me away. It’s taken the horribly geeky and incomprehensible syntax of IRC, and made it as easy to use as an AOL chat room. Beautiful and elegant design which just emphasise why Macs are such great machines.
