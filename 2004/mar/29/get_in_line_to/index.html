Get in line to get in line...

➠ March 29, 2004

Get in line to get in line…

… then take a number and wait to be called.

Anyone who thinks they’ve experienced bureacracy should try hanging out at the Immigration office.

There were plenty of tired and huddled masses around, but that’s because we were standing outside at 7am in the wind-tunnel-like environment of Government Center in Boston.

It was fascinating, listening to the stories of the other people in line. The words “since 9/11” were bandied around, as if a valid reason, by all kinds of people being there. The person behind me in the first line was a 70-year-old woman who’s held a Green Card for 40 years, but now has to renew it because of the changes since 9/11. Excellent work there, Dept of Homeland Security. We’ll flush those bastards out somehow.

After 30 minutes in line outside prior to the X-Ray machine, then another 30 minutes lined up in the lobby, I get to a human being. He takes my envelopes of paperwork, and without looking at them, sticks them inside a larger envelope. I don’t get a receipt. I have no kind of proof that I’ve submitted this huge wad of documents that determine whether I’m allowed to remain in the country or not. I can hope to receive one “within about 45 days”.

Hooray for Bush’s pledge to make the Immigration Services more efficient.
